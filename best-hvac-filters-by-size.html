Best HVAC Filters by Size

Updated April 2026 · 11 min read

Finding the right filter size is step one. Step two is picking a filter that actually works at that size. Not every brand makes every size, and quality varies wildly even within the same brand. Here’s our size-by-size breakdown with top picks for each.

How to Find Your Filter Size

Pull out your current filter and look at the label on the frame. You’ll see three numbers like 16x25x1 or 20x20x4. That’s length × width × depth in inches. If there’s no label, measure the filter yourself—round to the nearest inch. The “nominal” size (what’s printed) is always slightly larger than the actual dimensions. A 20x25x1 filter actually measures about 19.5 x 24.5 x 0.75 inches.

16x20x1 — The Most Common Size

This is the single most popular residential HVAC filter size. If you live in a standard 3-bedroom home built after 1990, there’s a good chance this is your size.

Best Overall

Filtrete 1500 MPR (MERV 12)

3M’s Filtrete line is the most trusted name in home air filtration for good reason. The 1500 MPR captures dust, pollen, mold spores, and pet dander without choking your HVAC system. It’s electrostatically charged to attract particles rather than just blocking them, which means better filtration with less airflow restriction. Replace every 90 days.

Less Common Sizes

If your filter is an unusual size (14x20x1, 14x25x1, 12x24x1, etc.), your best bets are Filtrete and Nordic Pure—both offer the widest range of sizes. Avoid buying a filter that’s close but not exact. Even a half-inch gap lets unfiltered air bypass around the edges, defeating the purpose entirely.
