Filter Basics in 60 Seconds

1. Find Your Size

Check the label on your current filter. Common sizes: 16x20x1, 16x25x1, 20x20x1, 20x25x1, 20x25x4. The numbers are length × width × depth in inches.

2. Pick Your MERV

MERV 8 is the minimum worth buying. MERV 11-13 catches pollen, dust mites, and pet dander. Don’t go above MERV 13 unless your HVAC can handle it—too much restriction reduces airflow.

3. Set a Schedule

1-inch filters: every 30-90 days. 4-inch filters: every 6-12 months. Pets and allergies = replace sooner. Set a phone reminder—your HVAC will thank you.
