⚠️ Filter thickness matters for price: Most homes use 1" filters ($5–$18 each). If your HVAC system takes 4" or 5" deep-pleated filters, expect to pay 2–3x more per filter. Check your existing filter's dimensions before ordering — a 20x25x4 is a completely different product than a 20x25x1. Deep filters last longer (often 6–12 months vs 1–3 months for 1" filters), so the per-month cost is closer than the sticker price suggests.

Best Filter for Your Situation

🤧

Allergies

MERV 11-13. Catches pollen, mold spores, and fine dust without starving your HVAC system.

→ Filtrete 1500 (MERV 12) or Nordic Pure MERV 12
🐕

Pets

MERV 10-11 and change every 60 days. Pet hair clogs fast — pleated filters help.

→ Filtrete 1000 (MERV 11) or Honeywell FPR 9
🏠

General Use

MERV 8 is the sweet spot. Good filtration, minimal airflow restriction, affordable.

→ Nordic Pure MERV 8 or Filtrete Basic
🔥

Smoke / Wildfire

MERV 13 minimum. Check your HVAC can handle the restriction. Consider a standalone HEPA purifier too.

→ Filtrete 1900 (MERV 13) or Nordic Pure MERV 13

🌀 Bottom Line

For most homes, a MERV 8-11 pleated filter is the sweet spot. Filtrete is the safest choice — consistent quality and widely available. Nordic Pure is the value pick — nearly identical performance at a better price.

Don't go above MERV 13 unless your HVAC manual specifically says it can handle it. Over-filtering restricts airflow, makes your system work harder, costs more to run, and can shorten its lifespan.

The most important thing is changing your filter on schedule. A dirty MERV 8 performs worse than a clean MERV 5.
